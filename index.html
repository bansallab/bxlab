A disease ecology and network science research lab

We focus on the interactions that occur between hosts for the transmission of infection. Examples include handshakes among humans, burrow sharing among tortoises, or synchronous breathing among dolphins. These interactions are often difficult to observe, and yet they are key drivers in both the ecological and evolutionary dynamics of infections disease.

Our goal is to understand how social behavior shapes infectious disease transmission and how knowledge of such processes can improve disease surveillance and control

We use both bottom up approaches (including contact/behavioral data collection, transmission experiments, and mechanistic models) and top down approaches (using epidemiological data and statistical models) to get at important social behavior and the role it plays in dynamics for a variety of human, wildlife, and livestock disease systems.
