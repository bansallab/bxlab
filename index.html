A disease ecology and network science research lab.

We focus on the interactions that occur between hosts for the transmission of infection.

These interactions are often difficult to observe, and yet they are key drivers in both the ecological and evolutionary dynamics of infections disease.

We use both bottom up approaches (including contact/behavioral data collection, transmission experiments, and mechanistic models) and top down approaches (including epidemiological data and statistical models) to get at important host interactions and the role they plan in dynamics for a variety of human, wildlife, and livestock disease systems.
