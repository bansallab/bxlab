Research

Our research is based in disease ecology (also known as ecological epidemiology). This includes population and community level stuides of the interactions between hosts and their pathogens, and covers diseases of both humans and animals. In particular, we focus on spatial and temporal heterogeneity in transmission, due to variation in host-dependent parameters (e.g contact patterns, age, or compliance with public health recommendations). We use and develop a variety of mathemtical and statistical modeling tools to answer our questions, in particular tools of network science.
